Julie Wyman

Responding to Change over Following a Plan: Agile Lessons from Antarctica

01:00 PM – Hanover

I spent January 2018 in Antarctica hanging out with penguins, whales, and seals. It was as different from my day-to-day work as can be. And yet, I couldn't help but reflect on how my trip aligned with a value of the Agile Manifesto: "Responding to change over following a plan."

It's a common misconception that there's no need to plan in Agile. While this isn't the case, and specific approaches do change, the act of planning still has great value when it occurs at the right level. Still, we accept that many things will change and we'll need to remain flexible.

Nowhere is this truer than in Antarctica. In this session, I'll share how my trip drove home why we need both planning AND, even more importantly, the ability to respond to change, and not just in software development. And after getting stuck in Antarctica, it also built up my empathy for team members struggling with dynamic situations!

KonMari Your Backlog: Tidying up those PBIs

Have you tidied your personal life with Marie Kondo and are wondering how to achieve the same effect at work? Are your most valuable product backlog items lost under a mountain of old stories? Join us to learn how to make your product better through the life changing magic of tidying your backlog.

Mixed Agile Arts: What can Agile Coaches learn from the UFC

10:45 AM – Chesterfield

Martial Arts have been around for thousands of years, and it takes practitioners decades to truly master an individual discipline. The UFC emerged in the early 1990s as a way to pit individual martial arts against another to determine which art was superior. As it turned out, the most effective method of combat was a blending of individual arts into what we today refer to as Mixed Martial Arts (MMA). While Agile isn't thousands of years old, it has given way to many different disciplines. You may have seen or been part of some fairly passionate debate on which Agile "art" is more effective. Scrum vs Kanban, SAFe vs LeSS, LeanUX vs Design Thinking. In this session, we will explore a Mixed Agile Arts approach to problem solving. Rather than debate the efficacy of one practice over the other, let's pull from many different frameworks to create a more dynamic approach to building better products, inspiring teams, and engaging everyone in a transformation.
